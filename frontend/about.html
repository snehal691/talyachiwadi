Our Story

Talyachi Wadi is more than a destination it is a living feeling of peace and belonging. The name itself carries its essence “Talyachi” comes from Talav, meaning lakes, inspired by the three man made lakes that brought life back to once barren land, and “Wadi” reflects the warmth and simplicity of a village, a space where community thrives. Together, it means “The Village of Lakes,” but in truth, it is a sanctuary where people celebrate silence, culture, and connection. Here, sunsets reflect on still waters, traditions are lived naturally, conversations flow without distraction, and the noise of the outside world gently fades away. Talyachi Wadi is not built to impress it is grown to nurture offering refreshment not just to the body, but to the mind and soul. Times Iconic Brands 2024 - Leading Agrotourism

Talyachi Wadi is more than an agro-tourism destination — it is a living celebration of rural Maharashtra. Born beside a serene “talya” (pond) in the heart of Nashik, this land once flourished quietly as farmland. Over time, it evolved into a welcoming space where families, children, and travelers could reconnect with nature, tradition, and authentic village life — without losing its original soul. Our Beginning The vision behind Talyachi Wadi was simple yet powerful: To preserve our roots in a rapidly urbanizing world. Instead of turning the land into another commercial project, the founder chose to nurture it — protecting the soil, supporting local farmers, and creating a space where culture and community thrive together. Every tree planted here carries intention. Every meal served carries heritage. Every guest who walks in becomes part of our extended family. Whether you are planning a family trip, corporate event, or wedding, we provide complete comfort and safety with professional management.

MEET FOUNDER

When I first stood on this land,

There was almost nothing here dry soil, open sky, and barely a few trees. No water, no shade, no life as you see today. But I did not see emptiness; I saw possibility. I didn’t want to build a resort. I wanted to heal the land. Because I believe when the land heals, people heal too.

So I began planting trees one by one. Over time, more than 10,000 trees and plants found their home here. We created three lakes where there was once no water. We nurtured rare medicinal plants into Charak Srushti, built Nakshatra Van to connect nature with the cosmos, and cultivated a Lotus Park with 150 species as a symbol of purity and renewal. Everything here was grown with patience and intention.

Talyachi Wadi was never meant to be just a tourist place. My dream was to create a sanctuary a space where you can hear a leaf fall, where families reconnect, where culture is lived naturally, and where silence is not empty but meaningful. If someone leaves this place feeling lighter, calmer, and closer to nature, then my vision is fulfilled. This is not just a destination; it is my gratitude to the earth and an invitation for you to be part of something that continues to grow. My dream was to create a sanctuary...

-Mr. Gopal Patil

Nakshatra Van

Nakshatra Van is a sacred garden inspired by ancient Vedic knowledge. In Indian astrology, there are 27 Nakshatras (constellations), and each one is connected with a special tree that carries spiritual and ecological significance.

At Talyachi Wadi, these sacred trees are planted to create a living forest that connects people with nature, astrology, and traditional wisdom. Visitors can walk through the garden and discover the relationship between stars, trees, and life on earth.
